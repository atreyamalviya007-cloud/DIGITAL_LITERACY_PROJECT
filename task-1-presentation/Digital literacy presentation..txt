Digital literacy

is the ability to effectively use digital technologies, understand online information, and engage responsibly in the digital world.
 For students, useful digital tools

 include note-taking apps, cloud storage, learning management systems, and collaboration platforms that enhance productivity and learning. Safe internet practices—such as using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and respecting privacy—are essential to protect personal data. Building a professional online presence through platforms like LinkedIn helps showcase skills and achievements, while maintaining 

proper email etiquette—using

 clear subject lines, polite language, and concise messages—ensures effective and respectful communication in academic and professional settings.
